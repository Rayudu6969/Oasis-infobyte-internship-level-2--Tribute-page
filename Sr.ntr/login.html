Nandamuri Taraka Rama Rao


INFORMATION :

Nandamuri Taraka Rama Rao(NTR) was a legendary Indian actor,filmmaker,and politician, primarily known for his work in Telugu cinema and for his significant contributions to politics in the state of Andhra Pradesh, India. Here's an overview of his life and achievements:

Early Life :

NTR was born on May 28, 1923, in Nimmakuru,a village in the erstwhile Madras Presidency (now in Andhra Pradesh). He belonged to a farming family.

Acting Career :

NTR's acting career spanned over five decades,during which he starred in more than 300 films, predominantly in Telugu cinema. He gained immense popularity for his portrayals of mythological characters like Lord Krishnaand Lord Rama,as well as for his roles in social dramas and action films. Some of his iconic movies include "Pathala Bhairavi","Maya Bazaar",and "Daana Veera Soora Karna".

Political Career :

NTR founded the Telugu Desam Party(TDP) in 1982,with the primary aim of safeguarding the interests of the Telugu-speaking people. He entered politics with the slogan of "Telugu vari Atma Gouravam" (self-respect of Telugus). In the 1983 Andhra Pradesh Legislative Assembly elections, TDP stormed to power, with NTR becoming the Chief Minister of Andhra Pradesh. He served three terms as the Chief Minister, from 1983 to 1989, from 1989 to 1994, and briefly in 1995.

Contributions as Chief Minister :

NTR implemented several welfare schemes during his tenure as Chief Minister, including the mid-day meal scheme for school children, the establishment of residential schools for underprivileged students, and the enhancement of the public distribution system.

Personal Life :

NTR was married to Basava Tarakam,with whom he had eight children. His sons Nandamuri Balakrishna and Nandamuri Harikrishna also became prominent actors and politicians.

Passing :

NTR passed away on January 18, 1996,leaving behind a rich legacy in both the worlds of Cinema and Politics.
